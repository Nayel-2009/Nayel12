About StandUpTogether

StandUpTogether explains what bullying is, shows the impact it has, and gives simple, relatable tools to help you recover. Our content uses everyday contexts like gaming and school to make learning feel familiar.

What Is Bullying?

Bullying is when someone repeatedly hurts, threatens, excludes, or mistreats another person on purpose. It can happen with words, actions, or online. Bullying is not a one-time disagreement or argument — it's behavior that happens more than once and creates an unfair power imbalance.

  • Physical bullying: Harm through actions like hitting or pushing.
  • Verbal bullying: Hurtful comments, insults, or name-calling.
  • Social bullying: Leaving someone out, spreading rumors, or damaging friendships.
  • Cyberbullying: Using phones, social media, or online spaces to embarrass or harm someone.

How Bullying Affects People

Bullying can have a big impact on someone's emotional and social well-being. Even if the actions seem small to others, the experience can stay in someone’s mind for a long time. Bullying can affect:

  • Confidence: Many people start to feel unsure about themselves.
  • School life: It can make people feel distracted, stressed, or uncomfortable at school.
  • Friendships: Bullying can make it hard to trust others or feel included.
  • Emotions: People may feel sad, worried, or alone when they’re dealing with bullying.

Why Preventing Bullying Matters

Stopping bullying is important because everyone deserves to feel safe and respected. Learning how to support others, speak up, and show kindness helps create a stronger, healthier community where people can grow and feel valued.
